Dream interpretation works best when the dream is recorded before the waking mind smooths out the strange details. Start with the scene: where you were, who appeared, what changed, and what emotion stayed with you after waking.

In DreamClue AI, a short entry is enough to begin. Write the dream in ordinary language. Do not worry about perfect structure. Fragmented notes such as "dark hallway, locked door, calm but watched" can still reveal useful patterns.

After saving the dream, use the interpretation view to compare the entry with related symbols and themes. The result should be treated as a reflection aid, not a fixed verdict. A door may suggest transition, privacy, fear, possibility, or something personal from your own memory.

The most useful practice is repetition. Record several dreams across a week, tag the mood, and review which images return. DreamClue AI becomes more useful when you can compare one dream with the wider pattern of your own journal.
