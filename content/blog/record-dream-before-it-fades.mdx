Dream memory is fragile. The first few minutes after waking often decide whether a dream becomes a useful journal entry or disappears into a mood you cannot name.

Keep the process small. Write one sentence about the setting, one sentence about the main action, and one sentence about the feeling. If you remember only an object, write the object. If you remember only the emotion, write the emotion.

Useful dream notes often include five details: place, people, movement, emotion, and strange symbols. A dream about water is different when the water is clear, rising, frozen, or impossible to cross.

Do not edit too early. Dream logic is often contradictory. Preserve the contradiction, because the odd transition may be the part that later explains the emotional pattern.

When you have time, add tags. Tags such as water, flying, family, door, school, fear, calm, or pursuit make it easier to review recurring images across weeks.
