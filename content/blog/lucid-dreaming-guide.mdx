Lucid dreaming begins with recognition: at some point inside the dream, you notice that the experience is a dream. Control may follow, but control is not the first goal.

The most practical preparation is dream recall. A person who remembers dreams regularly is more likely to notice repeated settings, strange transitions, and impossible details while dreaming.

Reality checks can help when they become a habit. During the day, pause and ask whether you are dreaming. Look at text twice, count your fingers, or notice whether the scene has a stable history.

Before sleep, review a recent dream and choose one clue that would have revealed it as a dream. This trains the mind to recognize similar clues later.

Lucid dreaming should remain gentle. If the practice increases anxiety or disrupts sleep, step back and return to ordinary dream journaling.
