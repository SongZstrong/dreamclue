Traditional dream dictionaries are useful because they collect recurring associations. They remind us that falling often relates to loss of control, teeth may connect with vulnerability, and doors often point to transition.

But a dictionary cannot know the dreamer. It does not know whether a school hallway felt safe or humiliating, whether a dog was beloved or frightening, or whether a train station appears in your life every time you face a choice.

DreamClue AI uses symbol references as a starting point and then asks the dream text to narrow the meaning. The emotional tone matters. The action matters. Your recurring history matters.

When a dictionary gives one answer, treat it as a prompt: where does that reading fit, where does it fail, and what personal detail changes the meaning?
